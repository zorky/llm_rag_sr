End-of-Life Policy

3DS OUTSCALE continuously strives to develop and improve its product offer. Eventually, certain products reach their end-of-life (EOL) state and are no longer sold, maintained, nor supported by 3DS OUTSCALE. To assist you in the transition process, different lifecycle phases have been defined.

Phases

Status Generic definition

End of Sale (EOS)

EOS is the date at which a product may no longer be available for purchase nor renewal.

As of this date, you can still use the product but the release of new features ceases. The SLA is still in effect, including the Technical Support.

3DS OUTSCALE informs current customers whether a successor product is available.

End of Support and Maintenance (EOSM)

EOSM is the date at which a product is no longer fixed nor supported.

As of this date, you can still use the product at your own risk. The SLA ceases to have effect, except for its guarantee of Sustainability which is effective until the EOL.

End of Life (EOL)

EOL is the date at which a product is no longer available for use. The product, including stored data, is removed from our Regions. Related information is deleted from our websites, including the documentation.

As specified in 3DS OUTSCALE’s General Terms and Conditions of Sale (GTCs), the client alone is liable from a legal and technical standpoint for its system (including the data, regardless of their origin).

Beta services are only subjected to the end-of-life state. For the definition of beta services, see the Glossary. For more information on beta services, see our General Terms and Conditions of Sales.
